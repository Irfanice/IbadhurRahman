    What is Holy Quran?

    The Quran, the last revealed word of God, is the primary source of every Muslim’s faith and practice. It lays down for them the law and commandments, codes for their social and moral behaviour, and contains a comprehensive religious philosophy. The language of the Quran is Arabic. It is a compilation of the verbal revelations given to the Holy Prophet Muhammad(sa) over a period of twenty three years.

    Indeed, [O Muhammad], you do not guide whom you like, but Allah guides whom He wills. And He is most knowing of the [rightly] guided. Quran (28:56)

    The Holy Quran

    The Quran is the last testament in a series of divine revelations from God (Allah in Arabic). It consists of the unaltered and direct words of God, which were revealed through the Angel Gabriel to Muhammad[PBUH], the final prophet of Islam, more than 1400 years ago.

    3 Oftenly using words

    BISMILLAH

    It means, “In the name of God.” This is often said before you start eating, or whatever before doing something. This is also said at the beginning of a new surah (chapter) of the Quran.

    ALHAMDULILAH

    It means, “Praise God” — and is often said when something good happens or even when something you were expecting to happen but still accepting and thankful of God’s will. It is also said after eating to thank God for the meal..

    Allahu Akbar

    "God is greater." It is a powerful declaration used by Muslims on many occasions and in many prayers.

    The 6 Beliefs of Islam

    1. One God

    Belief in the Oneness of God. Refer Surah Ikhlas 112th Chapter.

    2. Angels

    Belief in the Angels of God. The angel Gabriel brought the divine revelation to the prophets.

    3. Holy Quran

    Belief in the Books of God. Only the Quran remains as it was first revealed to the Prophet Muhammad[PBUH].

    4. Messengers of God

    Twenty-five of these prophets are mentioned by name in the Quran, including Noah, Abraham, Moses, and Jesus. Muslims believe that Muhammad is the last in this line of prophets, sent for all humankind with the message of Islam.

    5. Day of Judgment

    Belief in the Day of Judgment. Humans will be judged for their actions in this life; those who followed God’s guidance will be rewarded with paradise; those who rejected God’s guidance will be punished with hell.

    6. Divine Decree

    Belief in the Divine Decree. This article of faith addresses the question of God’s will.
